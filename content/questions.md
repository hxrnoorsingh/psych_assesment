# Internet-Induced Learning Difficulties Assessment
## Based on PDM-2 Framework for Adolescents (Ages 12-18)

## Axis MA: Mental Functioning
1. I find it hard to concentrate on schoolwork after using the internet for extended periods.
2. I often feel mentally exhausted after spending a lot of time online.
3. I have difficulty controlling how much time I spend on the internet.
4. I notice my attention span has decreased due to frequent internet use.
5. I struggle to reflect on my own thoughts and behaviors related to internet use.
6. I find it challenging to make realistic plans about limiting my screen time.
7. I have trouble switching between online activities and academic tasks.

## Axis PA: Personality Patterns
1. I prefer spending time online rather than interacting with family or friends.
2. I feel restless or irritable when I cannot access the internet.
3. I tend to avoid academic responsibilities by going online instead.
4. I notice my relationships with others have been affected by my internet use.
5. I feel that the internet has become my primary way of coping with stress.
6. I have developed patterns of internet use that interfere with my daily routine.
7. I find myself thinking about online activities even when doing other things.

## Axis SA: Symptomatic Distress
1. I have been feeling anxious or on edge, especially related to my academic performance.
2. I have felt down, depressed, or hopeless about my studies.
3. I have had trouble sleeping due to late-night internet use.
4. I have felt tired or had little energy affecting my school performance.
5. I have had trouble concentrating on reading or studying for extended periods.
6. I have felt overwhelmed by the pressure to keep up with both school and online life.
